What is Ventus?

In short, Ventus is a student management software, better know as our built in-house Accommodation Management application. It is designed to simplify, to streamline, and ultimately to decrease the work required of students with disabilities in receiving their approved academic accommodations. Ventus is also used to facilitate the sharing of accommodation-related information between all partners in the accommodation process. Partners include you, the Learning Specialist, Academic Accommodations support staff, Professors, Students, and Faculties.

As a Learning Specialist, you will be using Ventus as part of your day-to-day workflow. This is where you'll store all meeting notes, generate letters of support, and establish accommodation supports for students. It's important to remember that, like any software, Ventus is meant to help your day-to-day operations, not do the tasks for you.

If you're already familiar with the Ventus portal, click on the button below. This will bring you to the login page. If you're new to the role, take some time to watch the videos in the next section. These instructional videos will provide an overview of the most important features in the Learning Specialist portal.

Ventus Basics

This video is an overview of the non-student specific Ventus tabs in the LS portal. This includes the Calendar, Follow-ups page, Student list, Templates, and brief descriptions of the Hubs. You'll find that some pages are used more often than others. For example, you'll rely on the Follow-ups page to notify you of any student-specific updates.

As part of the new workflow, the Follow-ups page will include a notification each time a new student is assigned to you and every time a student schedules an appointment. Ventus calendars will also be used to track and schedule your meetings.

Student specific tabs are used much more frequently in the LS portal. These pages will only appear after having searched for a student in the top of page search bar.

Our last video dives into the action to take in order to establish an accommodation plan. It's important to keep in mind that these supports will not be displayed to professors until course-specific accommodation selection is done.
